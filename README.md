# 💊 High-Cost, Low-Utilization Drugs: Per Beneficiary & Per Claim Analysis

## 📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes **Medicare Part D data** to identify drugs that are both **high-cost** and **used by a small number of beneficiaries**. Using **pivot tables**, I examined:  

- 💵 **Cost per Beneficiary** – highlights the most expensive drugs per patient  
- 🧾 **Cost per Claim** – shows cost per prescription/claim  
- 📈 **Top drugs by spending in 2025** – identifies commonly prescribed, high-spending medications  

The goal is to highlight **rare, high-impact drugs** and understand which medications drive **Medicare spending**.

---

## 🔍 Key Findings
- Identified **top 30–35 high-cost, low-utilization drugs**.  
- Many specialty drugs cost **>$10,000 per beneficiary** but are prescribed to **fewer than 100 beneficiaries**.  
- Pivot analysis highlights drugs that, despite low usage, represent **significant per-patient costs**, which could be areas for cost management.  
- Some of the **common high-spending medications** among Medicare recipients include:  
  - 💊 **Eliquis** – widely used anticoagulant  
  - 💊 **Ozempic** – diabetes medication with high per-patient cost  
  - 💊 **Jardiance** – diabetes medication frequently prescribed to Medicare beneficiaries  
- Extreme outliers in both beneficiary and claim-level metrics were also identified, highlighting **rare specialty medications** with very high costs.
