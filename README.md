# 🏥 Medical Malpractice Claims Analysis

#️⃣ #HealthcareAnalytics #SQL #PublicHealthData #PolicyAnalysis #CostDrivers

---

## 🌟 Project Overview

- 🏥 Conducts structured analysis of medical malpractice claims data to evaluate patterns in **claim severity, payout distributions, medical specialties, insurance types, and legal representation**  

- 📊 Identifies key drivers of **healthcare cost variation and risk exposure** across clinical and demographic factors  

- 💡 Translates raw claims data into **actionable insights on healthcare spending behavior and liability risk trends**  

- 📌 Supports interpretation of findings relevant to **healthcare financing, insurance operations, and public health policy decision-making**  

---

## 📂 Dataset Variables

| Variable | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 💵 Amount | Total claim payment amount in USD |
| ⚠️ Severity | Injury severity scale (1 = emotional trauma, 9 = death) |
| 👤 Age | Age of claimant in years |
| ⚖️ Private Attorney | Indicates legal representation status |
| 💍 Marital Status | Marital status of claimant |
| 🏥 Specialty | Physician specialty involved in claim |
| 🧾 Insurance | Type of insurance coverage |
| 🚻 Gender | Gender of claimant |

---

## 🎯 Analytical Approach

- 🗄️ Built structured dataset using SQL tables to organize and prepare claims data for analysis  

- 🧹 Performed data quality validation to identify missing, inconsistent, or incomplete records  

- 📊 Conducted descriptive statistical analysis across insurance type, specialty, and demographic groups  

- 💰 Evaluated severity-weighted financial outcomes to identify cost variation patterns  

- 👥 Analyzed demographic and legal representation factors influencing claim outcomes  

- 📑 Aggregated results into summary tables to support interpretation of healthcare cost and risk drivers  

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- 💡 Claim severity is strongly correlated with higher payout amounts across cases  

- 🏥 Medical specialty is a major driver of cost variation and financial exposure  

- ⚖️ Legal representation is associated with measurable differences in claim outcomes  

- 📉 Significant variability exists across demographic and insurance categories
